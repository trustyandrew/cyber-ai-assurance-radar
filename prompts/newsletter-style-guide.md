# Newsletter style guide

## Voice

Independent, concise, useful, assurance-led. Not hype-driven, not alarmist, not a
generic cyber roundup. Australian English. Write as a trusted adviser who filters
signal from noise.

## Structure (target: under 1,000 words)

1. **Subject line options** — 3 variants.
2. **Preview text** — one line.
3. **Opening note** — 2–3 sentences naming the week's single clear theme.
4. **Top developments** — 3–5 items, each with one line on why it matters.
5. **Standards & assurance watch** — ISO/IEC 27000 & 42000 family movement.
6. **Responsible AI watch** — governance, transparency, oversight, AI risk.
7. **Cyber security watch** — ASD/ACSC, resilience, threat/vulnerability signals.
8. **Australian public sector & regulatory watch** — when relevant.
9. **Practical takeaway** — one concrete thing the reader can act on.
10. **Soft CTA.**

## CTA (preferred wording)

> If your organisation is preparing for ISO/IEC 27001, ISO/IEC 42001, cyber
> assurance or responsible AI governance, I can help you separate what matters from
> what is noise — and build an evidence-based path forward.

## Rules

- Not a dump of all dashboard items — curate.
- One clear theme per issue.
- Every item links to a primary source.
- No fabricated facts, standard numbers or publication claims.
